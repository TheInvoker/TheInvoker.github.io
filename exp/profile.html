  • Scotiabank HackIt Debt Challenge Hackathon

    February 2016 – February 2016

    Our solution "Debt-Less" (android/webapp) helps you manage your debt. Existing services out there such as Mint, analyzes trends in your transactions and presents analyzes on how much you spend per category and makes suggestions on how you can save. We felt services like this make too abstract and generic statements that can confuse people about what choices exactly to make to save.

    So we tried to tackle this problem by giving very custom and tailored suggestions to users. By analyzing transaction history and predicting the customer's future debt, we can figure out which exact products the user can save money on, and make suggestions to lower or limit how much they spend on it.

    The way we deliver it, is by issuing 3 challenges every week to the user. Each challenge can tell the user to buy perhaps 2 or 3 less of that product for this week. For example, if you buy coffee on average 5 times a week, a challenge could be to buy it 2-3 times at most this week. Or to spend a certain amount less than before. These automatically get validated through the transaction history or can be marked manually to complete challenges.

    The aim of the challenges, is to deliver direct unambiguous guidelines in a fun way, to the user such that if the user completed all the challenges, then their savings would significantly contribute towards their debt. The criteria of the challenges can be set with settings such as maximum time to pay off a debt or which categories do you want to ignore.

    Users can see on a graph which indicate their debt history, making it very easy to see their progress. Additionally we have a geo-fencing feature basically means if you have GPS enabled on your phone and happen to walk into a store that's relevant to one of your active challenges such as Starbucks for a coffee saving challenge, then you will get a notification reminding you of the challenge.

    Team members:
    • Ryan D'Souza,
    • Jason Du,
    • Fidelia Hung,
    • Jianan Han

    January 2016 – January 2016

    Have you been in a public place such as a library or coffee shop, with a laptop and other materials all out, then wanting to step away to buy something or use the restroom? Did you feel that you do not want to pack up everything because you don't trust the people around you to not steal your tech? Then this is an app for you!

    Today, we are often left vulnerable, and restless about our personal belongings - unable to monitor them past our own eyes. We wanted a world with more control given to the users - the people of technology. So we delivered "WatchMy.Tech", a quick, secure, easy-to-use service that lets you stream the camera feed of one device to another. Security and control are at the tip of your fingers with our service.

    Our app "WatchMy.Tech" is able to be run from an Android app or a website, connecting it through a secure, remote server.

    - allows you to monitor and deter any unwanted activity near your streaming device such as a laptop. You can view it on a mobile web app (website) or an Android app.
    - send messages (preset or custom) to your streaming device which are spoken out loud using native text to speech API.
    - receive automatic notifications when faces are detected in the video feed. Your phone will also vibrate.
    - feed capturing into images for later evidence.
    - Geo-location logging.
    - multi-feed support so you and other friends can all view the same live webcam feed.

    We hope that our system will help reduce theft and give some peace of mind to users around the world.
